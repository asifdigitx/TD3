TRANSMISSION & DISTRIBUTION SYSTEMS RELIABILITY & SYSTEM PROTECTION 

INTRODUCTION:

The Transmission & Distribution Systems Reliability & System Protection Workshop is a high-energy, facilitated experience that provides you cutting-edge insights into the intricacies of the reliability of the transmission and distribution system and the critical role system protection play in securing the grid and ensuring the reliability of the grid. Our residences and businesses rely directly on the distribution system and transmission system for our daily activities. COVID-19 stressed the importance of having a reliable power during blue and black-sky day. In order to ensure a reliable energy system, it is critical to understand various aspects that impact the distribution system in order to ensure the reliability and resiliency of the future 100% clean and renewable systems. The reliability of the distribution system varies whether the distribution circuits are overhead or underground. The distribution design plays an even bigger role, whether it is radial, auto loop, buried residential distribution or network. The utility’s reputation is measured by the total number of customers interrupted, total outage duration, number of interruptions, whether momentary or permanent in a given time. Typical distribution circuits do not have the same capability/reliability or length as typical transmission circuits. The transmission system reliability varies based on the transmission substation design and the design of the transmission circuit whether overhead or underground. This course will compare substation designs, impact of weather on the circuit reliability and common modes of failure. This course will introduce the 5-35 kV class known as the medium voltage system, the impact of different weather patterns on various distribution designs as well as the impact of various faults from a substation and circuit level. Various transmission voltages will be analyzed. This course will also analyze common reliability indices and how to improve them. Super storm Sandy stressed the importance of system protection and how the system went haywire when the relays were not available to provide their essential function. In order to ensure a reliable energy system, it is critical to understand the building block of the electric utility power system.

Climate change pose an unprecedented threat to the electric grid. Several states and countries have mandates for 100% clean and renewable energy systems by 2050 or sooner; it is paramount to understand the reliability of the transmission and distribution systems and the basics of system protection in order to ensure a smooth reliable and resilient transition.

 

Today’s utilities and energy-related industries need executives, engineers, and employees who understand the electric power system, who can effectively build strategies to design the 100% renewable and clean energy systems. To do this, leaders must have the necessary knowledge to make strategic decisions and to invest wisely in preparing for the energy transition, from the power plants to customers’ premises . In this course, you’ll analyze the reliability of the transmission and distribution systems and will be ready for the widespread transmission and distribution renewable interconnections and major climate-change transformation, as well as how renewables make proper system protection a challenging task, due to the backfeed, phantom load, etc.
